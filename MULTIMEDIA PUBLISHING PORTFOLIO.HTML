MULTIMEDIA PUBLISHING

As a student under the course of Multimedia Arts, it is indeed an experience that will bear such exploration of my artistic mind. It allowed me to be more capable of growth as I am penetrated by different types and forms of art that surrounds and impacts the current world that we live in. As I discover various waves of artistic media, I allow myself to find new interests and just the beauty of endless learning and discovering is something I will hold as I embark on my future career.

As someone who is deeply influenced by the pop surrealism movement, I incorporate this sense of strangeness and a flare of creative freedom within my school output. With the subject that I currently have in this 3rd year for this course, I am taking the opportunity to express my creativity in Multimedia Publishing.

Multimedia Publishing takes the designs in actual application and this essence of my creative designs having a purpose for the public. Think of it as producing artistic means and instruments that serve its purpose which is to convey a meaning and a way to deliver a message effectively.

Being an artist feels like an obligation to create something fruitful for myself and for the people who will potentially view these creative works. More so than not, there are so many aspects of being an artist that requires this deep relationship with one’s mind. For instance, there can be times when an artist experiences burnout and art block. I myself encounter this every time there is a great spark of passion then my mind and thoughts just struggle to come up with anything soon after.

To elaborate on what has been said so far, a deep relationship with your own mind does allow a connection to the abyss of possibilities that our mind holds. Perhaps we can say, it is a healthy way of knowing the limitations of your mind and how to take good care of one’s own mind.

In this chapter of my digital collection, I hope to showcase my current works from the prelim period which involves photo editing, poster designs, magazine designs, and social media campaigns.

 

 

 

EVENT POSTER DESIGN

In this poster design, the highlight was the creative works of a surrealist photography artist by the name Filip Custic. Whose works have been one of my inspirations for its strangeness and beautifully conceptualized creative shots that become an artistic piece of their own.

Much of this strangeness comes from combining elements that are oddly fitting but at the sametime conveys a meaning through those elements. As I design this poster, I keep in mind the approach that I will have for this design. What would feel aligned to the artist and the photography art that I will feature in this design?

This design really turned out neatly as I would have expected. Even on the mockup design, it does feel like something for its purpose. The information feels enough for the viewer’s perspective to know what the poster is for.

 

PHOTO EDITING DESIGN

Photo editing has to be one of the branches of multimedia arts that I really am interested to deeply learn. One reason being is that it can be used as creatively as possible and becomes an art piece of itself. For this photo editing project, I did use Spain’s devotion to Mary mother of God as an inspiration more specifically as this marching band vinyl cover design.

The photo was originally taken as a portrait image of the blessed Mary which I then enhanced the details by using contrast and blur that honestly did the job very well in making the bits of details look more neat.

Part of this design is also gathering more images that will be used as an element in the composition of the whole design. To keep all those elements coordinated and unified within one design, I made sure that the colors are all similar through adjusting the brightness, temperature, and saturation.

 

 

MAGAZINE LAYOUT DESIGN

I did recall creating a magazine design in the past school year under this course, it is safe to say that I did get to know the things to avoid when making a magazine design and the proper layout that makes the magazine design effective to its readers. For this magazine design task, I took inspiration from Japanese fashion magazines with a washed pastel color palette.

Upon making this design, I was able to also do photo editing for the photos that I gathered online. I did like how the front cover photo ended up looking for the character that blends well with the background that I picked. The inner pages have this very neat and clean format where the pictures pop up like a collage of cutouts with the text placed neatly all over the page.

The elements for this design involves Japanese text characters with y2k stars elements which did blend well. The placement of the text was carefully thought about for it can be messy to look at when not done properly. Overall, I have to say that this was a huge improvement in layouting as compared to my previous magazine designs from last school year.

 

 

SOCIAL MEDIA CAMPAIGN

Social media campaigns involve taking into consideration the layout and proper sizing of the public material that will be posted online. For this project in particular, we are tasked to create posts about an event in various social media platforms. I did come up with a campaign for health and art kind of event that I then composed a consistent design choice and route to accompany this said fictional campaign event.

The design that I went for in this social media campaign is that of a retro cartoonish and heavy texture overlays. I used bubbly fonts that are fun and cheerful to look at which perfectly fits the theme. My favorite elements of the design are definitely the cartoon characters in the form of food, planets, and devices. I think their adorable appeal brings the fun to the event and design.

The palette goes in this vibrant yet tamed look of pastel pinks, deep blue, light sky blue, and grass green. It is a flexible color palette that gives a good contrast within the designs. Playing around with those colors while keeping one dominant one to have consistency all throughout the design.
